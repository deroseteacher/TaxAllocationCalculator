Editorial Policy

TaxGal is an educational public finance project. The goal is to help taxpayers understand how their tax dollars may connect to public services, budget priorities, public debt, and government accountability.

Last Updated: May 2026

Editorial Principles

  • Use plain language so public budget information is easier for non-specialists to read.
  • Prefer official public sources, including budget offices, treasury departments, legislative fiscal offices, public accounts, audit reports, and local finance documents.
  • Explain uncertainty clearly when exact tax-dollar tracing is not possible.
  • Avoid partisan endorsements. TaxGal may discuss transparency, accountability, waste reduction, and civic participation without supporting a candidate or party.

How Content Is Updated

Calculator pages and source notes may be updated when better official data becomes available, when a model needs clarification, or when a reader identifies an error. Updates are intended to improve clarity, source quality, and usefulness.

Advertising And Independence

If TaxGal displays advertising, advertising does not determine the calculator methodology, editorial conclusions, source preference, or correction decisions. Educational usefulness and source transparency come first.
